<p class="header_box" id="description">Description</p>
<p>In 2007, David H J Bunka et al. used the SELEX method to isolate the aptamer with high compatibility for the Amyloid-like fibrils formed in vitro from monomeric beta-2-microglobulin (β2m). WL-2 binds to WL and LS fibrils with an on-rate that is much faster than the binding of LS-5 to the same fibrillar targets , suggesting that although these aptamers recognize an epitope present in both WL and LS fibrils, the epitope for each aptamer must be either distinct or differentially accessible in the different fibril forms<sup>[<a href="#ref6" style="color:#520049">6</a>]</sup>.<br></p>


<p class="header_box" id="SELEX">SELEX</p>
<p>In 2007, David H J Bunka et al. have exploited the power of RNA selection techniques to isolate small, structured, single-stranded RNA molecules known as aptamers that were targeted specifically to amyloid-like fibrils formed in vitro from beta(2)-microglobulin (beta(2)m), the amyloid fibril protein associated with dialysis-related amyloidosis<sup>[<a href="#ref6" style="color:#520049">6</a>]</sup>.<p>
